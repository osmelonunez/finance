# v3.5.0 - Accounts and Cards 2.0

## Goal

Turn accounts and cards into safe, inspectable entities connected to movements and banks without changing the database schema.

## Navigation

- Accounts, cards, and banks move out of Management into their own `/payment-methods` workspace.
- Internal navigation is split into `KPI`, `Relationships`, `Banks`, `Accounts`, and `Cards`.
- `/payment-methods` opens the KPI view by default.
- Legacy routes under `/management/payment-methods` and renamed routes redirect to their current destinations.

## Management and integrity

- Deletion is explicitly blocked when linked movements exist.
- The existing foreign key remains as a second protection layer.
- A new account or card can only be linked to an active bank.
- A bank relationship is also required when editing an account or card.
- An account or card cannot be activated while its bank is inactive.
- Deactivating a bank automatically deactivates its accounts and cards.
- Movement creation and duplication only accept active accounts/cards.
- Editing preserves an already-used inactive account/card but cannot assign a different inactive one.
- One contextual form creates a bank, account, or card and routes the request to the matching section.

## Details and history

The account or card detail view shows:

- type, status, bank, and reference;
- all-time spending;
- current-month spending;
- linked movement count;
- movements paginated in server-loaded groups of ten;
- a direct link to the filtered expense list.

The bank detail view shows its KPIs, accounts, cards, and the same paginated movement history.

The `KPI`, `Relationships`, `Banks`, `Accounts`, and `Cards` tabs remain visible from every detail view.

## Filters, KPI, and relationships

- The expense list includes separate bank, account, and card filters.
- Filters include inactive entities so historical records remain accessible.
- The KPI view shows active bank, account, and card counts together with current-month spending.
- The financial summary switches between banks, accounts, and cards and supports annual year selection.
- Separate monthly, annual, and total spending charts are complemented by one combined comparison chart.
- Each chart displays a clear empty state when the selected scope and period contain no spending.
- The `Relationships` view groups accounts and cards under their bank and sorts banks by linked item count.
- The former bank summary table was removed to avoid duplicating KPI and chart information.

## Amount presentation

- Large amounts and counts use locale-aware digit grouping.
- Charts use the same monetary format for axes, visible labels, and tooltips.

## Database

No migration is required. The implementation uses existing relationships and indexes on `payment_methods`, `banks`, and `records`.
